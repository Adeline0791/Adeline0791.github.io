Reichstagsgebäude

The German Parliament Building built in 1894 was the site of the National Assembly during the German Empire and the Weimar Republic. During the split between the two Germanys, the post-war restored parliament building was located in West Berlin, but at this time the Federal Republic of Germany's parliament had moved to the capital, Bonn.
After the reunification of the two Germans, in order to regain the role of the Capitol, the Capitol building experienced another round of re-construction: adding a special glass dome. Besides, "DEM DEUTSCHEN VOLKE" on the west gate of the Capitol means "for the German people."
Address:Platz der Republik 1, 11011, Berlin TIERGARTEN
Opening hours:Dome and rooftop tour (with audio guide): every 15 minutes from 8:00 to 24:00, the last admission time is 22:00.
Tickets:FREE

Museum Island

Museum Island in Berlin is a small island that brings five museums together, which is built between 1824-1930. Due to the commemorative significance of culture and architecture, it was designated a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1999. The five museums are: the old museum in Berlin and the new museum, the National Gallery, the Bode Museum and the Pergamon Museum. Museum Island brings together the best of German museums, such as the Pergamon Museum, which shows the history of ancient Egypt; the National Painting Gallery, which collects the works of world masters.
Address:Museumsinsel (Bodestraße) 10178 Berlin
Opening hours:Closed on Mondays Tuesday to Sunday 10:00-18:00; Thursday 10:00-20:00
Tickets:There is a one-day pass for the Museum Island, the fare is 18 euros. The discount ticket is 9 euros. The museum pass and the tickets for the museums can also be booked online, there will be some discounts.

East Side Gallery

his is the longest and most famous Berlin Wall preserved in Berlin with 1.3 km in total. In 1990, the Berlin Wall was torn down. After the reunification of the two Germanys, the legacy of the Berlin Wall became a paradise created by contemporary artists. It was dominated by political themes and experienced a renovation in 2009. This Berlin Wall is the most visited section of the three remaining Berlin Walls.
Address:East Side Gallery, Mühlenstraße, 10243
Tickets:free

Berliner Dom

Built between 1894 and 1905, Berliner Dom is used to be a royal church with extremely magnificent decoration. It is decorated with columns with complicated lines and exquisite murals, and even the stigma is gold-plated. The building has 4 floors and the highest point is 114 meters. Berliner Dom is a Christian Lutheran church in Berlin, Germany. It is located in the eastern part of the Museum Island in the city of Berlin. It was once the court church of the Hohenzollern dynasty of the German Empire.
Address:Am Lustgarten 1, 10178 Berlin MITTE
Opening hours:Monday – Saturday 09:00–20:00, Sunday and other public holidays 12:00–20:00 Winter (October 1st - March 31st) Closed at night 19:00
Tickets: Adult:EUR 7; Student: EUR 4; Church introduction brochure: 20 euro cents

Brandenburger Tor


The Brandenburg Gate is a symbol of Berlin and a national symbol of Germany. The Brandenburg Gate faces towards east, which is Berlin's Old Town. It is also the only remaining Berlin gate between East Berlin and West Berlin. The Brandenburg Gate has stood here for more than two hundred years and has witnessed the rise and fall of German history. During the split of the East and West Germany, the Berlin Wall was erected on the west side of the Brandenburg Gate, and no one passed it for decades.
Address:Pariser Platz, 10117 Berlin.
