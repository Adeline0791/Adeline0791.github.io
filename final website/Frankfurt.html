Frankfurt

Frankfurter Römer

The Frankfurter Römer is the town hall square in Frankfurt since the Middle Ages, it stands in the heart of Frankfurt's Old Town. The square is located in the north of the Main River Bridge, it is a beautiful residence built in the late Renaissance style (1600). The church is built with red sandstone. On the east side there is a wooden rib wall house built from the 15th to 18th centuries. On the north side there is a stone house (1464) built in the style of an Italian palace. In the center of the square stands the Fountain of Justice (Gerechtigkeitsbrunnen), which was built in 1543. In 1944, the square was violently attacked by the British Air Force and almost completely destroyed. Then, it is rebuilt after the war.
Address:Roemerberg 27, 60311 Frankfurt, Hesse, Germany
Opening hours:All day
Tickets:FREE

Cathedral Historical Frankfurt

This is one of the most famous churches in Frankfurt. It was built in 1239 for St. Barito Lomois and it became the official venue for the election of the Holy Roman Empire in 1365. Ten emperors were crowned here during 1562 to 1792. On August 14, 1867, a fire destroyed almost the entire church. The church was later built into a neo-gothic building, but it was destroyed again during World War II, and the current building was built in 1950-53. In summer, you can buy an ice cream cone and sit in front of the church for a while to enjoy the slow pace life in Europe.
Address:Domplatz 14,60311 Frankfurt am Main
Opening hours:Church: Monday to Thursday 9:00-20:00, Friday 13:00-20:00, Saturday-Sunday 9:00-20:00. Church Museum: Tuesday to Friday 10:00-17:00, Weekend: 11:00-17:00 Monday: Closed.
Tickets:The church is free. Adult ticket for the church museum: 4 euros; discount price: 2 euros. The price includes a guided tour fee starting at 3pm daily.

Römer

The Frankfurt City Hall was the place of election and coronation of the emperor. It is a building that combined three medieval-style conjoined private residences and located in the old city of Frankfurt, Germany. The whole building is named because of Zum Römer, which is the oldest and most luxurious house among these three. The City Hall is now the location of the registry and the mayor’s office. As the earliest government office in Frankfurt, the building became city hall since 1404 and restored to its original appearance in the 1980s. It is a typical German-style building and one of the symbols of Frankfurt.
Address:Römer, 60311 Frankfurt am Main, Germany
Opening hours:Monday-Sunday: 10:00-13:00, 14:00-17:00.
Tickets:Adult/ Sepcial offer: 2/1 Euro.

Eurotower

The European Central Bank has three offices in Frankfurt. The Euro sign in front of the ECB and the Euro logo that often appears on television is Eurotower, a symbolic building of Frankfurt, one of the European financial centers.
Address:Eurotower, Kaiserstraße 29, 60311 Frankfurt am Main, Germany
