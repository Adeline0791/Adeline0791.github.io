Miniatur Wunderland

This miniature world in Hamburg is the world's largest railway model scene and is still under construction. Covering an area of 1,300 square meters, it remotely controls 930 analog railway lines and covers almost all train lines in the world at a ratio of 1:87. Last year, the Miniature World celebrated its 10th anniversary and received a total of 10 million visitors. Visitors shuttle between 8 rooms through a long ramp. Each room is about 100 to 300 square meters with a certain theme. For example, Austria, the United States named after the country; the Swiss Alps and Scandinavian landscape named after the landscape.
Address:Kehrwieder 2-4/Block D, 20457 Hamburg
Opening hours:Monday to Friday (except Tuesday) 9:30-18:00; Tuesday 9:00-21:00; Saturday 8:00-21:00; Sunday and public holidays 8:30-20:00.
Tickets:Adults: 15 euros per person; children aged under 16: 7.5 euros

Port of Hamburg

The Port of Hamburg, also known as the Hafen City, is situated on the banks of the Elbe River, close to the North Sea. It is the largest port in Germany and has a gateway to the world. Not only is it an important project in Hamburg's municipal construction, it is also Hamburg's most famous tourist destination. On the banks of the River Elbe, you can find the various boats, delicious snacks, as well as the aquarium and the famous hamburger fish market.
Address:Steinwerder
Opening hours:Monday-Sunday 9:00-18:00
Tickets:8.50 to 9 Euros.

Elbphilharmonie

The Elbphilharmonie is a concert hall in Hamburg, Germany. It is part of the Speicherstadt, located at the western end of it. Elbphilharmonie is the tallest residential building with 7 floors height.The concert hall is open to everyone else.
