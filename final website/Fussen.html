Neuschwanstein Castle

Neuschwanstein has undoubtedly become a landmark for German tourism, and it is the most famous castle in Germany for most tourists. Neuschwanstein is located on the summit of the southern border of Germany, on a hilltop nearly a kilometer high. This is a masterpiece that Ludwig II has fully utilized its national strength to build up the castle with its fairy-tale looks and fantasitic scenery.
When you stand on the bridge of Marian, you can't help falling in love with it.
The interior of Neuschwanstein is also extremely graceful, but after all, it is impossible to cover up the tragic fate of Ludwig II.
According to legend, Ludwig II had a keen interest in the legend of the knight, and after staying in Wartburg in Thuringia, he decided to reproduce the scenes of the knight legend in his castle. Neuschwanstein was built in 1869. 17 years later, when Ludwig II died, the facade was just completed. Three months after the death of the king, the government transfered it as a museum. At present, the interior of the castle can be visited, including the throne hall, the king's bedroom, the living room, the dressing room, the big concert hall, etc. The decoration is very gorgeous.The location of Neuschwanstein Castle is in the vicinity of the small town of Füssen. If you want to stay around here, Füssen is a good choice. It is also an ancient town that brings you back to the Middle Ages.
