Dresden

Frauenkirche

Founded in 1726, the Cathedral was built with the arches and sandstones for 17 years in an unprecedented way. The Frauenkirche is 95 meters high, and it is a masterpiece of the new Western church architecture. It is the landmark of this ancient city and the most beautiful scenery in Dresden. There are many music masters and art masters here.
Address:Neumarkt, 01067 Dresden
Opening hours:Monday to Friday 10:00-12:00, 13:00-18:00, Saturday 9:00-15:00
Tickets:FREE

Dresdner Zwinger

It is the most attractive Baroque building in Germany. This palace is mainly a place where the royal family holds a dinner.
Zwinger Palace is the most magnificent and famous ancient building in Dresden. It is the highest-honered masterpiece of the architect Berman, completed in 1732. In the 19th century, the famous architect Semper, who designed the Semper Opera, added an Italian Renaissance-style part to the north.
Address:Sophienstraße, 01067 Dresden
Opening hours:Tuesday to Sunday 10:00-18:00
Tickets:The palace courtyard can be visited free of charge, and the museum will be charged separately.

Der Fürstenzug

Dresden has a 101-meter-long porcelain mural - Der Furstenzug, which traces the time of the Wettingin Dynasty. The king was vividly displayed in the painting as the form of horseback riding. The murals show the horse-riding trips of thirty-five rulers (including fathers and sons and brothers) in Sachsen from 1127 to 1876 (the lower end of the statue is marked with their name), along with accompanying scientists, artists, artisans, soldiers, children and farmers.
Address:Taschenberg 2 01067 Dresden
Opening hours:All day
Tickets:Free
