Heidelberger Schloss

On the blue Königstuhl, the Heidelberg Castle is built by the red sandstone. Although it is almost destroyed, the castle still retains the king's demeanor, overlooking the old town of Heidelberg and the Neckar River at the foot of the mountain.
The castle was built in the 13th century. It was struck twice in 1537 and 1764, and was severely damaged in the 30-year war.
Today, the biggest attraction in the castle is the world's largest Grosses Fass, which is also the fourth barrel at similar size repeatedly built in the castle because of the war. Today's barrels were built in 1751 and can hold a total of 221,726 litres. There is also a medical museum in the castle that shows the pharmacy hundreds of years ago. There is a red Arc de Triomphe in the courtyard of the palace. Although the gate is small, it was built overnight in a quite romantic way. This is the birthday gift of Friedrich V to his wife in 1615 when she was 20 years old.

Alte Brücke

The official name of the old bridge is the Karl-Theodor Bridge, which is also the name of the electorate who built the baroque stone bridge in 1788. This is the ninth bridge in this position since ancient Roman times and the oldest bridge on the Neckar River in Heidelberg. The bridge is a pedestrian bridge and you can look up to the castle from here. The southern end on the bridge is the sculpture of elector Karl Theodor, and the northern end is the goddess of wisdom. The southern end of the bridge is the bridge gate.The bridge gate was originally used to collect the “bridge fee” but it is free now.

Hauptstraße

The main street is the most important street in the Old Town of Heidelberg, from the Bismarckplatz in the west to the Karlsplatz in the east. This 1.6-kilometre pedestrian street has the main shopping and dining venues in the old city and is one of the longest pedestrian streets in Europe.
The main street is narrow and sturdy. It was built up in the Middle Ages, but the buildings on both sides were mostly destroyed by the Great League War during the French invasion period in the late 17th century. The houses built later were mostly Baroque, now it is an outstanding example of the late Renaissance architectures.
Address:Hauptstraße 22, Heidelberg

Philosophenweg

The philosophenweg, also known as the philosopher's path, is a two-kilometer long walking route on the north side of the Neckar River in Heidelberg. Because of its pleasant scenery and quiet atmostphereand, it is suitable for thinking andhas been appreciated by professors, scholars and philosophers in history. They often squat in this place to capture the inspiration of thought. Now, the trail is still a good place to hang down the old town of Heidelberg.
